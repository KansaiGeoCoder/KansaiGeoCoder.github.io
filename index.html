Urban form, proximity, and the everyday city.

From Master’s Thesis — Kyoto University, 2023

Shotengai — reimagining walkable urban corridors

Across Japan’s cities, Shotengai — the covered shopping streets lining neighborhoods — represent more than commercial infrastructure. They are the connective tissue of daily life: linking homes, transit, and local identity through walkable proximity.

This study, conducted as part of my Master’s research at Kyoto University, explores how these arcades function as spatial indicators of urban vitality. By mapping various walkability factors in Kyoto, it examines the relationship between built form, everyday movement, and economic resilience. The findings contribute to a broader understanding of how local morphology supports liveable, human-scale cities.

About

Northlayer Studio

I’m Maximilian Prutsch, a geographer and cartographer working across Austria and Japan. I hold an MSc in Environmental Management from Kyoto University, a BSc in Geography from the University of Graz, and a GIS Certificate from the University of Salzburg.

My work focuses on spatial analysis, urban research, and cartographic design, exploring how people interact with the built environment, from walkability studies to Shotengai revitalization projects.

Drawing from both European and Japanese planning contexts, I aim to create maps that bridge analytical depth with visual clarity, tools that help cities, researchers, and communities make informed, human-centered decisions.
